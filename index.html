Abstract

Automatic anomaly detection is an crucial task in video surveillance systems for improving public safety. Many approaches used two-stream networks, which included a spatial stream and a temporal stream to exploit both spatial and temporal information in videos. However, optical flow requires a heavy computation in temporal stream. In this paper, we introduce a deep and efficient spatial-temporal Network (DesNet) for video anomaly detection. Features of video frames are exploited both spatial and temporal information at the same time. Furthermore, the contextual dependencies of features are computed by spatial-channel attention. We evaluate the proposed DESNet on three benchmark datasets: USCD Ped2, CUHK Avenue and ShanghaiTech. Experiment results show that DESNet achieves the state-of-the-art performance with 97.4\%, 86.6\% and 73.6\% on USCD Ped2, CUHK Avenue and ShanghaiTech, respectively. The project page of this paper can be found at https://tuan-lv.github.io/desnet/.
